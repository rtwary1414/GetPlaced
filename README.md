# GetPlaced
The objective of this application is to provide a structured roadmap that will help students to have market expected skills. It saves time and solves the problem of lack of direction by using Llama 3.2 to personalize learning paths, estimate timelines and track progress efficiently. 

Introduction: 

My proposal for an Android application is a career guidance tool which will be designed by me to help students plan, and track their journey towards their goals of getting a job. Once a student graduates, they are eligible for multiple roles like Software Engineers, Data Analyst, Game Developer, etc. While preparing for interviews, it becomes really difficult for students to study everything hence a tracker will help them to stick to what the market wants which is the USP.

Objective: 

The objective of this application is to provide a structured roadmap that will help students to have market expected skills. It saves time and solves the problem of lack of direction by using Llama 3.2 to personalize learning paths, estimate timelines and track progress efficiently. 

Description: 

The proposed application is an Android-based AI career guidance and progress tracking tool for students preparing for different job roles such as Software Engineer, Data Analyst, Game Developer, and Business Analyst. The app will provide structured role-based roadmaps so that students can focus only on the skills, tools, and topics that are most relevant to their target career.

The main user interactions will begin from a role selection screen, where students choose their desired job path. After this, the app will show a career dashboard containing required skills, recommended subjects or courses, estimated completion time, and progress percentage. A task tracker screen will allow users to mark modules as completed, view pending topics, and monitor how much of their preparation is finished in terms of percentage. An AI guidance screen will let students ask questions such as what to study next, how long a skill may take, or which topic is more important for a selected job.

Llama 3.2 will be used as an on-device AI assistant for personalised study planning, question answering, roadmap generation, summarisation of learning tasks, and simple recommendations. This improves personalisation, reduces manual planning effort, and supports faster decision-making. Running the model on-device improves privacy, offers low latency, and allows limited offline support. Basic safety controls will include prompt restrictions, refusal of harmful or unrelated requests, response length control, and keeping student progress data stored locally unless the user explicitly allows syncing.

On-Device: It will run the AI model locally on the phone, ensuring low latency, better privacy, and offline capability.
Local Server: It processes the AI tasks on a nearby system mainly for development or testing of larger models.
Cloud Endpoint: It uses remote servers for powerful processing but depends on the internet and may raise latency and privacy concerns.

Conclusion: 

I will use the Agile/Scrum approach to develop this application. I will divide this project into small modules, so that continuous improvement of both core features and AI integration is possible. It seems suitable for Llama 3.2 as it supports quick handling of uncertainties, and regular feedback from users to refine accuracy, usability and performance.
